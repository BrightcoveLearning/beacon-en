Destinations

Create destinations to publish videos to YouTube, Facebook and Twitter.

Create a YouTube destination

To create a YouTube destination, follow these steps:

  1. Click Destinations in the left navigation.
  2. Click Add Destination.
  3. Click Sign in with YouTube.
  4. You will be prompted to login to your Google account (if not already signed in). If you have multiple accounts, make sure you login to the account associated with your YouTube channel.
  5. A dialog will open requesting permission to manage your YouTube account. Check the upper right of the dialog to confirm you are using the correct Google account.
  6. Click Allow.
  7. Give the destination a unique, descriptive name. Click Add.
  8. Click the Edit button in the BASIC INFO section to edit the destination name. See the Setting YouTube defaults section for information on configuring default values.
  9. Click Destinations in the left margin and confirm that the destination appears in the list of destinations. The value below the destination name is the destination ID.

Create a Facebook destination

To create a Facebook destination, follow these steps:

  1. Click Destinations in the left navigation.
  2. Click Add Destination.
  3. Click Sign in with Facebook.
  4. A dialog will open requesting you to log into your Facebook account. Enter your credentials and click Log In.
  5. A dialog will open confirming that Brightcove Social will receive your profile info. Click Continue.
  6. A dialog will open confirming that Brightcove Social will be able to manage and publish your pages and access your Page and App insights. Click OK.
  7. Give the destination a unique, descriptive name. If you have multiple Facebook pages, select the page to publish to using the dropdown list and then click Add.
  8. Basic information and the destination defaults will be displayed. Click the Edit button in the BASIC INFO section to edit the destination name. See the Setting Facebook defaults section for information on configuring default values.
  9. Click Destinations in the left margin and confirm that the destination appears in the list of destinations. The value below the destination name is the destination id.

Create a Twitter destination

To create a Twitter destination, follow these steps:

  1. Click Destinations in the left navigation.
  2. Click Add Destination.
  3. Select Sign in with Twitter.
  4. You will be prompted to login to your Twitter account and authorize Brightcove Social to use your account. Login to the account you want to post videos to. If you are not prompted to login, you may be logged into a Twitter account. Make sure you are logged into the correct account before proceeding. Click Authorize App.
  5. Give the destination a unique, descriptive name. Click Add.
  6. Basic information and the destination defaults will be displayed. Click the Edit button in the BASIC INFO section to edit the destination name. See the Setting Twitter defaults section for information on configuring default values.
  7. Click Destinations in the left margin and confirm that the destination appears in the list of destinations. The value below the destination name is the destination id.

Delete a destination

To delete a destination, follow these steps:

  1. Click Destinations in the left navigation. A list of destinations will appear.
  2. Click the destination name to delete.
  3. The destination details will be displayed. Click the delete icon and then click Delete to confirm the deletion.
    4. Note: The delete icon will not appear if there are Auto Sync jobs created that use the destination. You must remove the Auto Sync jobs before deleting the destination.

Reconnect to a destination
Social will display an error message if any destinations have issues. This can happen if the Brightcove permissions are removed from the social media account or when the credentials used when creating the destination are no longer valid.

To resolve the issue:

  1. Click the destination name.
  2. Click the Reconnect link at the top of the page.
  3. You may be prompted to re-authenticate with the social media account.
